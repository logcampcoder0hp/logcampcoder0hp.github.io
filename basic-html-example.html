The page of a Babylon 5 fanatic

This site is about the TV show . It was a really cool show. The characters were very real and well developed. A whole lot happens in the show. There are several wars, some going on at the same time. Some took place before the series takes place, but were referenced because of their impact to the present. The Dilgar war, the Minbari war, the Narn-Centari war, just to name a few.

There are other characters not listed, some of whom are major characters in the series, but the above list, I think, covers the characters who I like. But my top five characters are:

  1. Londo Mollari
  2. G'Kar
  3. Michael Garibaldi
  4. Alfred Bester
  5. Vir Cotto

I like Londo Mollari because although he starts out as an amusing character who is always running around, late for an appointment because he is always loosing the time (and his money) at the casino, he changes and becomes (SPOILER ALERT) a very dark character who is instrumental in causing the Centauri to become a big bully race. But even when he watches the Narn homeworld being bombarded, he looks extremely unhappy. He nevertheless plays the role he must at every turn of his life and drinks right down to the dregs of the cup, and he does everything very well; he knows how to be an excelient ambassador and how to accomplish what needs to be done to replace the emperor for the sake of the Centarui people. I think that what I really like about him is that his feelings on what he would like to do are so different from what he feels that he must do and therefore does.

G'Kar on the other hand, I like him because his emotions are more closely correlated with his actions. He is not in so many situations where he feels that he must do things because of social consequences, he does what he does because it is in his interests, which normally includes the interest of the Narn people.

Michael Garibaldi is a great character, because has personal problems, most of which stem from his occasional years struggling with alcohol.

Alfred Bester Can be cold as a snake, and bite as severely, but even he shows that he has human soft-spots and higher goals in life that motivate him to be extremely shrewd. Also, he has a handicap, one of his hands is lame. One wonders how he puts the gloves on.

Vir Cotto has some kind of inexplicable innocence. He is too unimportant to kill, or generally be noticed. Hmm, now how might that be handy?
